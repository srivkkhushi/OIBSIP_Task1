Figure out your home financing

Your financial health determines your readiness to purchase a home. A lender will evaluate your credit score, income, debts and history of paying your loans to help you identify a budget for your home purchase. This is called mortgage pre-qualification.

Why get pre-qualified

  • Calculate an affordable mortgage budget before house hunting
  • Get familiar with mortgage lenders
  • Surface qualifying down payment assistance programs

Hire a real estate agent

A buyers agent is a licensed real estate agent that works on your behalf, guiding you through the process of buying a home, surfacing homes within your budget and helping you draft a competitive offer.

Why work with a buyers agent

  • Gain access to homes promptly, tour at your convenience
  • Win sellers attention by making agent-informed offers
  • Commission is often paid for by home sellers
  • Save money on your final purchase through competitive negotiation

Begin shopping for homes

Complete the majority of your home shopping online with filtered or saved searches, and detailed property pages to limit the number of properties you need to visit in person. Filter your search by price range, home type and size.

Why shop for homes online

  • Receive personalized notifications for listings
  • Explore remote home tours, layout diagrams, professional photographs and maps to get a feel for the property and surrounding areas
  • Review property details, taxes, local insights and amenities
